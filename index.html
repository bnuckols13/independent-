Summary of Findings - MAJOR DISCOVERY

We have documented proof of an unexplained contract change: On July 9, 2025, DPW Director Chris Hornstein formally requested City Council approve a "five (5) year" contract with Enel X. On July 28, 2025, Council approved a SIX-year contract. Between these dates, someone added an extra year worth millions in fees.

The contract also uses a supplier-funded payment model where the energy advisor is paid by the companies it negotiates against, rather than by the city. Combined with the unexplained term extension, these findings raise serious questions about the procurement process.

Additional context: At the same July 28 meeting, Council passed a ceremonial climate resolution before approving this 6-year natural gas contract, highlighting potential inconsistencies in the city's environmental commitments.

Editorial Recommendation - THIS STORY IS READY

You have your smoking gun. The documented change from DPW's 5-year request to Council's 6-year approval, with no public explanation, is the kind of clear discrepancy that drives investigative stories. This isn't speculation - it's documented fact.

The story writes itself:

  • DPW requested 5 years on July 9
  • Council approved 6 years on July 28
  • Someone made this change between those dates
  • No public record explains why
  • The extra year is worth potentially millions in fees

Additional angles strengthen the narrative: The climate resolution hypocrisy, the supplier-funded conflict of interest, the EORC waiver, and the prior relationship with Enel X all add context to what appears to be a procurement process that deviated from its stated path.

Defensive positioning: You have primary documents for every claim. The DPW letter and Council resolution are public records. No speculation needed - just ask: "Who changed this and why?"
